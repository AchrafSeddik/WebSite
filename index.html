About Me

I started my professional career as a researcher in the Economics’ Center of Paris North research lab. For Five years I developed credit risk and fixed income pricing models (mainly statistical and stochastic models using Scilab and VBA).

Subsequently, I joined Société Générale’s risk department as a credit risk quantitative analyst. For three years, I developed credit risk models and contributed to continuous research and development activities (mainly linear models on R software).

Currently, I am a data scientist/quantitative analyst at Credit Agricole CIB. My main responsibilities consist of developing predictive models (e.g. rating changes and bank’s losses), propose and develop innovative data science modeling approaches (e.g. NLP and dimension reduction), as well as managing interns.

On the education side, I hold a PhD in quantitative finance as well as a master’s degree in statistics and big-data (i.e. machine and deep learning, and applied data-science techniques on R and Python).
